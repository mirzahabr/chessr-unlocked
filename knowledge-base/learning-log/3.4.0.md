## Reverse-engineering observations
- unlock-manifest.json's locate.anchors were seeded with post-unlock strings for 5 of 7 original entries -- corrected to pre-unlock anchors this run after discovering the mismatch during Phase 08
- apply_unlocks.py's detect vs verify subcommands answer different questions (pre-unlock locate vs post-unlock confirm) and had been conflated in the audit pipeline design -- corrected in pipelines/audit.md and capabilities/unlock-detection.md
- deobfuscate.sh had a bug: prettier could not infer a parser from an extensionless mktemp temp file, causing 100% false-failure on the first restoration attempt -- fixed by appending .js to the temp filename
- Raw-vs-restored structural signature diffs are not meaningful for a freshly-obfuscated build -- obfuscator.io hides addEventListener/fetch/setInterval/etc. behind computed member access, which webcrack correctly resolves to literal calls during restoration. The meaningful comparison is restored-vs-restored across versions, not raw-vs-restored within a version.
- Two accounts/Discord store unlocks were already applied in v3.3.2's shipped build and documented in PROJECT_REPORT.md prose, but had never been captured as their own unlock-manifest.json entries -- formalized this run (accounts-store-stub, discord-store-stub)
- AI move-explanation and Game Review known limitations (server-side dependency on a real Supabase session) persist unchanged from v3.3.2 -- not re-tested against the live backend, per the standing policy against probing third-party production services
